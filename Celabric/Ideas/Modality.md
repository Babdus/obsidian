# Modal prepositions

| Modaility    | Preposition | Original meaning | Example                              | Translation              | Literal translation    |
| ------------ | ----------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------ | ---------------------- |
| Ability      | **[[uf]]**  | from             | **[[ter]]** **[[uf]]** **[[xjar]]**  | I am able to walk        | Walking from me        |
| Necessity    | **[[osh]]** | for              | **[[ter]]** **[[osh]]** **[[xjar]]** | I need to walk           | Walking for me         |
| Permission   | **[[ef]]**  | to               | **[[ter]]** **[[ef]]** **[[xjar]]**  | I am allowed to walk     | Walking to me          |
| Requirment   | **[[a]]**   | on               | **[[ter]]** **[[a]]** **[[xjar]]**   | I must walk              | Walking on me          |
| Wish         | **[[ush]]** | by               | **[[ter]]** **[[ush]]** **[[xjar]]** | I want to walk           | Walking by me          |
| Inability    | **[[ysh]]** | unlike           | **[[ter]]** **[[ysh]]** **[[xjar]]** | I am not able to walk    | Walking unlike me      |
| Nonnecessity | **[[y]]**   | outside of       | **[[ter]]** **[[y]]** **[[xjar]]**   | I have no need to walk   | Walking outside of me  |
| Prohibition  | **[[yzh]]** | except           | **[[ter]]** **[[yzh]]** **[[xjar]]** | I am not allowed to walk | Walking except me      |
| Freedom      | **[[yf]]**  | without          | **[[ter]]** **[[yf]]** **[[xjar]]**  | I am allowed not to walk | Walking without me     |
| Undesire     | **[[ozh]]** | opposite of      | **[[ter]]** **[[ozh]]** **[[xjar]]** | I hate to walk           | Walking opposite of me |
When changing the tense, aspect or mood of the modals, you should change the those categories of the verb itself: **[[terxt]]** **[[osh]]** **[[xjar]]** _"I needed to walk"_.

Several modal prepositions can stack between verb phrase and noun phrase: **[[ter]]** **[[osh]]** **[[ozh]]** **[[xjar]]** _"I hate that I need to walk"_. This can be represented as ((**[[ter]]** **[[osh]]** \[**[[xjar]]**\]) **[[ozh]]** **[[xjar]]**) _"(I hate that (I need to walk))"_.
